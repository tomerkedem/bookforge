# Examples of Classic Machine Learning Uses

In this course we will focus on Generative AI, but it's important to understand that classic Machine Learning also has a central and irreplaceable role in many systems.

Not every problem is suitable for solving with generative models. In many cases, when the task is well-defined and requires a precise and fast decision, classic models are the better choice.

The reason for this is deeper than a technological difference.
Classic Machine Learning does not focus on creation, but on identifying patterns within data and inferring decisions based on them.

In fact, most real-world uses rely on a limited number of recurring patterns:

**Identification, prediction, ranking, personalization.**

<img src="/ml-lesson-2-summary/assets/image-04.png" alt="image-04.png" width="404" height="220" />

These are not just types of problems, but ways of thinking.

Almost every Machine Learning-based system can be broken down into one of these patterns, and sometimes into a combination of them.

**To understand this, we can examine several common examples:**

• Fraud detection or network intrusion detection is a case of anomaly detection within a data stream.

• Search results and advertisements are ranking problems, where it must be determined what will be displayed first.

• Product or content recommendations are a case of personalization for the user.

• Risk assessment or price determination are prediction problems based on historical data.

• Image recognition is a classification case, which is a specific form of identification.

• Predicting system failures is another use of the prediction pattern, but in an operational context.

The examples vary, but the structure remains constant.

**This is exactly the power of Machine Learning:**

Not the ability to solve one problem, but the ability to identify a recurring structure and apply it to a wide variety of problems.
